    How the score is calculated

    Vivid sunrises and sunsets need a canvas of high (cirrus) and mid‑level (altocumulus / altostratus) clouds for the low‑angle sunlight to bounce off, but a clear horizon in the direction of the sun so the light can actually reach those clouds. Low clouds near the observer block the show.

    For each sunrise/sunset, we pull the hourly forecast from Open‑Meteo (no API key) and combine these signals into a 0–100 score:

    • Mid‑level cloud cover (weight 30%) — best around 30–60%.
    • High cloud cover (weight 25%) — best around 30–70%.
    • Low cloud cover (weight 20%) — penalty above ~30%, the lower the better.
    • Humidity at surface (weight 15%) — lower humidity = cleaner colors.
    • Visibility (weight 10%) — higher = more vivid.

    Sunrise/sunset times come straight from Open‑Meteo's daily astronomy fields, which are computed from the standard NOAA solar position algorithm — so the timing itself is astronomical and accurate to the minute. The "best window" is roughly sunset − 25 min through sunset + 25 min (and the symmetric span around sunrise) — that covers golden hour through early blue hour.

    Data: api.open-meteo.com for weather, geocoding-api.open-meteo.com for city search. Both are free and CORS‑enabled.
